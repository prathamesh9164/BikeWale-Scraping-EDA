§02  —  Key Findings

Four Insights from the EDA

Structural patterns discovered across price, engine, brand, and consumer behaviour dimensions.

01 Price Analysis

Price Distribution is Heavily Right-Skewed

The majority of motorcycles on BikeWale fall in ₹50k–₹1.5L, creating a strong right skew. Superbike outliers push the mean (₹6.95L) far above the median (₹1.99L) — making median the better measure of the typical buyer's market. 162 models sit in the Mid-Range tier alone.

162 Mid-Range models 102 Luxury models Mean 3.5× Median
02 Correlation

Engine Capacity is the Strongest Price Predictor

With a Pearson r = 0.77, engine displacement predicts price better than any other variable. Mileage inversely correlates with price (r = 0.04) — confirming the classic performance-vs-economy trade-off baked into the market structure.

r = 0.77 (engine ↔ price) Superbike: 122 models Mid-cc fastest growing
03 Segmentation

Four Distinct Market Segments Emerge

3D scatter, bubble charts, and parallel coordinates all reveal the same four natural clusters: commuters (Hero, Honda, Bajaj), mid-range tourers, adventure/cruisers, and performance superbikes (Ducati, KTM, BMW). Each cluster has distinct brand loyalty and rating behaviour.

Commuter cluster Sports cluster Mid-range sweet spot
04 Brand Intelligence

Brand Portfolio Signals Untapped Market Niches

Pivot heatmaps show most brands specialise in 1–2 price tiers. Bajaj dominates by reviews and popularity; Ducati commands the highest average price; VLF holds the highest average rating. Consumer satisfaction plateaus across all tiers — ratings are segment-agnostic.

Ducati — Most Expensive Bajaj — Most Popular VLF — Highest Rated
